Momo

Description

The word “momo” is a phonetic transcription of the Tibetan word མོག་མོག (pronounced as mog mog). Historically, it is believed that the dish spread from the Kathmandu Valley of Nepal to Tibet or vice versa. One theory suggests that Nepali Newar merchants took the recipe from Tibet, where they traded, and brought it home to Nepal. Another theory is that a Nepalese Newari princess introduced momos to Tibet after her marriage to a Tibetan king in the late fifteenth century.

Ingredients

  • All-Purpose Flour (Maida): 1 cup (125 grams)
  • Oil: ½ teaspoon (for the dough) and 1 tablespoon (for the filling)
  • Salt: ¼ teaspoon (or as required)
  • Water: 3 tablespoons (for kneading the dough)
  • Small-Sized Spring Onions (Scallions): 2 (finely chopped; reserve the greens to be added later)
  • Garlic: ½ teaspoon (finely chopped, or 3 to 4 small-sized garlic cloves, finely chopped)
  • Mix Vegetables: 1¾ cups (finely chopped; a mix of cabbage, carrots, French beans, capsicum, and onions)
  • White Button Mushrooms: ½ cup (chopped, optional)
  • Soy Sauce: 1 teaspoon (or add as required)
  • Black Pepper Powder: ½ teaspoon (or as required)
  • Salt: To taste

Steps

  1. **Prepare the filling**: Finely chop vegetables like cabbage, carrots, bell peppers, and onions. Sauté garlic and ginger in oil, then add the vegetables and cook until tender. Season with salt, pepper, and soy sauce.
  2. **Make the dough**: Combine all-purpose flour, water, and salt in a bowl. Knead until smooth, then let it rest for 30 minutes.
  3. **Shape the momos**: Divide the dough into small balls and roll each into a thin circle. Place a spoonful of filling in the center, fold and seal the edges tightly.
  4. **Steam the momos**: Arrange the shaped momos on a greased steamer tray and steam over boiling water for 10-12 minutes, until cooked through.
  5. **Serve and enjoy**: Serve hot with a dipping sauce made from soy sauce, vinegar, chili sauce, and minced garlic. Enjoy your delicious vegetable momos!
