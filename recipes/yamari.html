Yamari

Description

Yamari is a traditional Nepali delicacy, particularly popular during the festival of Yomari Punhi. It consists of a sweet dumpling made from rice flour dough, filled with a mixture of sweetened sesame seeds, molasses, or other sweet fillings like coconut or khoya (reduced milk solids). The dumplings are then steamed until cooked and served warm. Yamari has a soft, doughy texture on the outside and a sweet, flavorful filling on the inside, making it a beloved treat during festive occasions in Nepal.

Ingredients

  • Rice flour
  • Sesame seeds
  • Molasses or sweeteners such as sugar
  • Coconut (optional)
  • Khoya (optional)
  • Water

Recipe

  1. Prepare the dough: Mix rice flour, water, and a pinch of salt to form a smooth dough. Let it rest for about 30 minutes.
  2. Make the filling: Combine grated coconut, sesame seeds, sugar, and cardamom powder in a bowl. Mix well to form the filling.
  3. Shape and steam: Take small portions of the dough, flatten them, and add a spoonful of the filling. Seal and shape them into elongated ovals. Steam until cooked through.
